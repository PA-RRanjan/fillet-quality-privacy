Privacy Policy for Fillet Quality App

Effective Date: June 30, 2026  |  Last Updated: June 30, 2026

Fillet Quality Smart Phone Application ("the App," "we," "our") is developed by The Conservation Fund Freshwater Institute for use in aquaculture fillet quality inspection. This Privacy Policy explains how the App handles data when you use it on your smartphone.

1. Summary

Fillet Quality application (App) is designed to run entirely on your smartphone device. It does not collect, transmit, or store any data on remote servers, and does not share data with us or any third party. All image processing and analysis performed by the App occur locally on your phone. Any sharing of data generated by the App (such as inspection images or results) happens only if you, the user, choose to share it yourself — for example, by email or another app of your choosing.

2. Information We Do Not Collect

The App does not collect, request, or store any of the following:

  • Personal identifying information (name, email address, phone number, etc.)
  • User accounts, login credentials, or authentication data
  • Location data or GPS coordinates
  • Device identifiers used for tracking or advertising
  • Usage analytics or behavioral tracking data
  • Crash reports or diagnostic data sent to external servers

3. Operational Data Processed by the App

In normal use, the App processes images of fish fillets to perform quality assessments, including color scoring and defect detection (e.g., gaping and melanosis). This data is:

  • Captured and processed entirely on your phone using on-device machine learning models
  • Not automatically transmitted to any cloud service, API, or third party
  • Stored locally on your phone's own storage, under your control
  • Not associated with any individual person's identity
  • Shared externally only if you actively choose to do so — for example, by emailing or exporting results through another app on your phone. The App itself does not initiate any sharing or transmission of this data.

This operational image and inspection data relate solely to product quality assessment and contain no personal information about any individual.

4. Camera Permission

The Fillet Quality App requires access to your phone's camera in order to capture images of fish fillets for color and defect analysis. This permission is used solely to take photos in the App for fish fillet quality inspection. The App does not access your camera in the background, does not use it for any purpose other than image capture, and does not automatically transmit camera images off your device. You can manage or revoke camera permission at any time through your phone's settings; doing so will prevent the App's core inspection features from functioning, since they depend on capturing images.

5. User-Initiated Sharing

The App may allow you to export or share inspection images and results using your phone's built-in sharing features (such as email, messaging, or file transfer apps). Any data shared this way is sent only at your direction, through the method you choose, and is subject to the privacy practices of the email provider, messaging service, or app you use to share it — not this Privacy Policy. We recommend reviewing the privacy practices of any service you use to share data.

6. Data Storage and Security

Because all processing happens locally on your phone, the security of any data generated by the App (such as saved inspection images or scoring results) is governed by the security of your device. We recommend applying standard mobile security practices, such as using a device passcode, keeping your phone's operating system up to date, and being mindful of which apps or services you use to share exported data.

7. Children's Privacy

The App is an operational tool intended for use by trained personnel in aquaculture and food processing settings. It is not directed at children, and it does not knowingly collect any data from children, as it does not collect personal data from anyone.

8. Third-Party Services

The App does not integrate with third-party analytics, advertising, or automatic data-sharing services. Any third-party tools used during development (such as model training infrastructure) are used solely offline for building the App's detection models and play no role in the App's data handling once deployed on your phone.

9. Changes to This Policy

If the App's data practices change in the future (for example, if optional cloud features are added), this Privacy Policy will be updated accordingly, and the "Last Updated" date above will reflect the most recent revision. We encourage users to review this page periodically.
